Guide · Finnish Berries
Finnish Berries for Life
🫐 · Nordic life · Once you taste a Finnish berry, there's no going back
Nordic life
Wild. Small. Extraordinary.

Finnish berries are not like supermarket berries. They grow wild in forests across the country, and the flavour — intensified by the long days and cold nights — is completely different from anything cultivated. In June the season is just beginning, and the first berries start appearing at markets.

Strawberries first — small, red, intensely sweet Finnish jordbubbar that make everything else taste of nothing. Then blueberries and lingonberries later in summer. Cloudberries — amber-coloured, growing in bogs — are the rarest and most Finnish of all. Find them as jam at the market and take a jar home.

Early summer · June
Finnish Strawberries
Mansikat. The first ones appear in late June and Finns treat them with genuine reverence. They are small, irregular, sometimes oddly shaped — and they taste of the most concentrated strawberry you've ever experienced. Buy them at the market. Don't refrigerate. Eat immediately. Do not put cream on them unless you're offered it by a Finn, in which case: yes.
Peak of June
🫐
Midsummer onwards
Wild Blueberries
Mustikka — wild blueberries that stain your tongue purple-blue and taste nothing like the large, watery cultivated kind. They'll just be starting in late June. Buy a punnet if you see them. Eat them plain, or with yoghurt, or as filling in a mustikkapiirakka — Finnish blueberry pie. The pie in particular is a national treasure.
Anna's favourite?
🟡
The rarest · July onwards
Cloudberries · Lakka
Amber-gold berries that grow in bogs in the far north, available in Helsinki mainly as jam. They taste like nothing else — a mix of raspberry, apricot, and something purely Arctic. Find cloudberry jam at the market or the Old Market Hall. It's expensive and worth it. Take a jar home. It belongs on vanilla ice cream or a good cheese.
Most Finnish thing
🔴
Autumn · but jams in June
Lingonberries · Puolukka
Small, tart, bright red — the lingonberry is Finland's other great berry and it appears on every table in some form. Fresh ones come in autumn, but the jam is everywhere year-round. It goes with meatballs, with game, with porridge. Take a jar home. It keeps forever and tastes of Finland every time you open it.
