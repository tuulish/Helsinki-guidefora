Nordic life
Wild. Small. Extraordinary.

Finnish berries are not like supermarket berries. They grow wild in forests across the country, and the flavour — intensified by the long days and cold nights — is completely different from anything cultivated. In June the season is just beginning, and the first berries start appearing at markets and in the forests.

But no worries even if the berries aren't ripe in June yet, every supermarket carries finnish frozen wild berries which are just as good.
