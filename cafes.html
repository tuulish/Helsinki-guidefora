Helsinki Cafés
☕ · Slow mornings · From Japanese sandos to century-old patisseries
Slow mornings
Helsinki takes its
coffee seriously.

The café scene moves between worlds: Japanese-inspired sando spots, grand old patisseries open since the 1800s, and specialty roasters treating single origins like fine wine.

Start the morning slowly. A cardamom bun or a fluffy fruit sando somewhere sunlit. No rush in the mornings.

Where to go
Six cafés
✦ Curator's note
"If you see a queue outside Café Tampopo, join it. Or get a Munkkipossu — the next best thing to a Korvapuusti — at least once.P.S. Possu means piglet :)."
Japanese Café · Kamppi
Café Tampopo
A tiny Japanese-inspired café next to Cinema Orion, serving sando sandwiches on cloud-light shokupan bread — chicken katsu, tamago, seasonal fruit. Matcha lattes, coffees, Hojicha, Swiss rolls, Purin pudding. Jazz records playing in the background. Almost always has a queue outside. Every minute is worth it.
📍 Eerikinkatu 15–17
Must Visit
Artisan Bakery · Töölö & Punavuori
Layers Bakery
Probably the best croissants in Finland. A tiny bakery run by Finnish-Spanish baker Daniel Tobal Autiokari — room for two people inside, a queue on the pavement. The pain au chocolat has perfect structure: light, buttery, crunchy outside, soft within. Go early on weekends. They're sold out by 11am.
📍 Cygneauksenkatu 6 · also Bulevardi 15
Go early
Grand Café · Since 1852
Café Ekberg
The oldest surviving café in Helsinki, open since 1852. Cake stands, pressed-tin ceilings, the smell of butter and something sweet baking. A place that feels both timeless and completely alive. Order the afternoon smörgåsbord or just a slice of something impossibly good. This is old Helsinki at its finest.
📍 Bulevardi 9
Since 1852
Coffee & Gelato · City Centre
Robert's Coffee Gelato Factory
A Finnish family-run café that does something unusual well: exceptional coffee alongside proper Italian-style gelato with Nordic twists — rhubarb, cloudberry, salted liquorice. The Gelato Factory on Aleksanterinkatu has the full spinning gelato bar. A brilliant afternoon stop, especially after a long walk around Senate Square.
📍 Aleksanterinkatu 21
