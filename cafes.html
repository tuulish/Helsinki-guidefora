Café Culture
☕ · Slow mornings · From Nordic roasters to century-old patisseries
Slow mornings
Helsinki takes its
coffee seriously.

Finland drinks more coffee per capita than almost any country on earth, and Helsinki is where that culture peaks. The café scene moves between two worlds: the grand old Viennese-style bakeries that have been here for over a century, and the cool Nordic third-wave roasters who treat a single origin like a fine wine.

Both are essential. Start the morning slowly — a cardamom bun and a flat white somewhere sunlit. This is not a city that rushes its mornings, and neither should you.

Where to go
Cafés & bakeries

Grand Café · Since 1891
Café Fazer
One of the most beautiful cafés in Helsinki, in business since the 19th century. Marble counters, chandeliers, glass cases full of pastries. Their Karl Fazer chocolate is legendary across Finland. Order the open sandwiches and something sweet and don't rush.
Anna will love this
☕
Specialty Coffee · Punavuori
Kaffa Roastery
Helsinki's pioneering specialty roaster, tucked into the Punavuori design district. The space is small, warm, and full of people who care deeply about what's in their cup. Buy a bag of beans to bring home. Their single origins are exceptional.
Must Try
🥐
Bakery · Kallio
Levain
A beloved neighbourhood bakery known for their sourdough and pastries. The kind of place that feels like it belongs to the people who live nearby — unpretentious, warm, genuinely good. The morning queue is worth joining.
Local Gem
🌿
Café · Esplanadi
Café Ekberg
The oldest surviving café in Helsinki, open since 1852. Cake stands, pressed-tin ceilings, the smell of butter. Order the afternoon smörgåsbord or just a piece of something impossibly good. A piece of old Helsinki that's still worth every visit.
Since 1852
🫐
Café · Lasipalatsi
Café Aalto
Inside the iconic Academic Bookstore designed by Alvar Aalto himself. The light through the skylights in June is extraordinary. A great place to sit with a book and a coffee and feel very European. The building alone is worth the visit.
Architectural gem
🏖️
Summer Terrace · Eira
Café at Kaivopuisto
Helsinki's café terraces come alive in June. Kaivopuisto park has a lovely open-air café right by the sea. Order something cold, find a table under the trees, watch the harbour. On a sunny June evening this is possibly the best place in the city.
