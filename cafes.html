Guide · Food
Food Culture
☕ · Culinary · From Nordic roasters to century-old flavours
The art of
Helsinki
on a silver platter.

Finland’s food culture is a conversation between the wild and the refined. From the morning’s first Korvapuusti in a sunlit bakery to the bustling market squares where savoury goods and local berries take center stage for lunch.

It’s a dialogue between light-filled summers and the heritage of the winter preserve. This restraint meets modern craft—from harbor-side cafés to wild, foraged flavors that follow the rhythm of nature.

Grand Café · Since 1891
Café Fazer
One of the most beautiful cafés in Helsinki, in business since the 19th century. Marble counters, chandeliers, glass cases full of pastries. Their Karl Fazer chocolate is legendary across Finland. Order the open sandwiches and something sweet and don't rush.
Anna will love this
☕
Japanese · Kamppi
Café Tampopo
Cozy Japanese-inspired café serving specialty coffee & tea, fluffy “sando” sandwiches, and sweet treats in a laid-back, music-filled setting.
Must Try
🥐
Bakery · Töölö
Layers Bakery
Small artisanal bakery known for flaky, buttery croissants, high-quality pastries, and specialty coffee, often with a takeaway-focused vibe.
Local Gem
🌿
Café · Esplanadi
Café Ekberg
The oldest surviving café in Helsinki, open since 1852. Cake stands, pressed-tin ceilings, the smell of butter. Order the afternoon smörgåsbord or just a piece of something impossibly good. A piece of old Helsinki that's still worth every visit.
Since 1852
🫐
Café · Lasipalatsi
Café Aalto
Inside the iconic Academic Bookstore designed by Alvar Aalto himself. The light through the skylights in June is extraordinary. A great place to sit with a book and a coffee and feel very European. The building alone is worth the visit.
Architectural gem
🏖️
Summer Terrace · Eira
Café at Kaivopuisto
Helsinki's café terraces come alive in June. Kaivopuisto park has a lovely open-air café right by the sea. Order something cold, find a table under the trees, watch the harbour. On a sunny June evening this is possibly the best place in the city.
