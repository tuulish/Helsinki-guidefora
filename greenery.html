Parks & Greenery
🌿 · Breathe · Helsinki turns impossibly green in June
Breathe
June in Helsinki
is impossibly green.

Helsinki is one of Europe's greenest capitals — built around parks, forests, and the sea. In June, after the long winter, everything is newly, almost aggressively green. The parks overflow. People are outdoors at midnight because the sun barely sets.

There's a particular Finnish relationship with outdoor space — parks are not decorative, they're essential. These are not tourist destinations, they're where the city actually lives.

Park · Sea · Eira
Kaivopuisto
Helsinki's grand park by the sea — large, hilly, full of old trees, with views of the islands and passing ferries. The park runs down to rocky coastline you can sit on. A café sits at the edge. On a June evening, with a long sky and the Baltic below, it is one of the best places in the entire city to simply exist. The kind of park that turns hours into minutes.

Urban Waterway · Töölö
Töölönlahti Bay
An urban bay in the centre of the city, ringed by a running and cycling path. Swans on the water, the opera house on one side, Finlandia Hall on the other. Walking the full circuit takes about 40 minutes and is deeply pleasant — especially at dusk when the water turns gold. Pedal boats available in summer if the mood strikes.
