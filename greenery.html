Parks & Greenery
🌿 · Breathe · Helsinki turns impossibly green in June
Breathe
June in Helsinki
is impossibly green.

Helsinki is one of Europe's greenest capitals — the city is built around parks, forests, and the sea. In June, after the long winter, everything is newly, almost aggressively green. The parks overflow. People are outdoors at midnight because the sun barely sets.

There's a particular Finnish relationship with outdoor space — parks are not decorative, they're essential. Esplanadi fills with office workers at lunchtime. Kaivopuisto is where people go to simply be. These are not tourist destinations, they're where the city lives.

Park · City Centre
Esplanadi
The long, tree-lined boulevard running from the city centre to the harbour — Helsinki's living room. In June it's full of people: families, musicians, café tables spilling out, children. The Kappeli restaurant at the far end has the city's best outdoor terrace. Walk it slowly, at least once.
Essential

Park · Sea · Eira
Kaivopuisto
Helsinki's grand park by the sea — large, hilly, full of old trees, with views of the islands and passing ferries. The park runs down to rocky coastline you can sit on. A café sits at the edge. On a June evening, with a long sky and the Baltic below, it's one of the best places in the city.

Urban Waterway · Töölö
Töölönlahti Bay
An urban bay in the centre of the city, ringed by a running and cycling path. Swans on the water, the opera house on one side, Finlandia Hall on the other. Walking the full circuit takes about 40 minutes and is deeply pleasant. Pedal boats available in summer if the mood strikes.
