Harbour & Islands
⛵ · On the water · Helsinki from its most beautiful angle
On the water
Helsinki belongs
to the sea.

The city was built on the edge of the Baltic, and you feel that everywhere. The harbour is the heartbeat — ferries, wooden boats, the smell of salt and fish. In June, the light never quite goes away, and the water glitters in that particular Nordic way that doesn't translate to photographs.

The surrounding archipelago has thousands of islands, many reachable by public ferry for the price of a bus ticket. This is one of Helsinki's great secrets — you can be on a wild, pine-fringed island within 20 minutes of the city centre.

⛵
UNESCO World Heritage · Island
Suomenlinna
An 18th-century sea fortress spread across six islands, just 15 minutes by ferry from Market Square. Part living neighbourhood, part open-air museum, part wildly beautiful park. In June the grass is impossibly green and the old stone walls have been warm with sun. Bring a picnic.
Must See
🏖️
Beach · West Helsinki
Hietaniemi Beach
The city's favourite urban beach, a short tram ride from the centre. In Juhannus week it fills with people soaking up the endless light. Sandy, unpretentious, very Helsinki. Grab an ice cream, find a spot, and just watch the Baltic.
Slow Day
⚓
Harbour · City Centre
South Harbour
The great wide square at the water's edge where the city and the sea meet. Ferries to Estonia, fishing boats, stalls selling smoked fish and strawberries. The Presidential Palace on one side, the Orthodox cathedral on the hill above. This is the postcard view, and it earns it.

🌲
Island · Nature
Pihlajasaari
A favourite local escape — two rocky islands connected by a bridge, with a sandy beach, café, and the kind of bare-rock Nordic nature that makes you understand why Finns love the outdoors so much. Summer ferry from Merisatama harbour. Swimming, sunbathing, complete exhale.
Local Gem
🚢
Ferry · Experience
The HSL Ferry Network
Helsinki's public ferries accept the regular transit ticket — meaning for the price of a bus ride you can hop between harbour islands. The JT-Line runs summer routes to several islands. It's a tiny, quiet revolution. Best value in the city, genuinely.
Insider Tip
🌅
Evening · Juhannus
Midnight Sun Walk
At Juhannus the sun barely sets. Walk the South Harbour at 11pm — the light is pink and gold and completely surreal. The city goes quiet except for occasional music from somewhere. This is the kind of thing you remember for years. Just walk. No plan needed.
