Guide · Markets
The Markets
🐟 · Taste Helsinki · Strawberries, smoked fish, cardamom buns, pickled things
Taste
Helsinki's markets
are alive.

The open-air market on the South Harbour has been here since the city's earliest days, and it still feels essential. In June it's at its peak — Finnish strawberries, smoked whitefish wrapped in paper, vendors calling out in Finnish and Swedish and tourist-English.

The indoor halls nearby are different — more refined, but no less vital. Cheese, bread, reindeer meat, cloudberry jam. Come hungry. Stay as long as you want.

The places
Markets & halls
✦ Curator's note
"Buy a paper cone of strawberries from the harbour market. Eat them walking. This is the correct way to do Helsinki in June."
Indoor Market Hall · Since 1889
Vanha Kauppahalli
The Old Market Hall, a red-brick beauty built in 1889. Inside: cheese merchants, fishmongers, spice sellers, a beloved oyster bar, and some of the best smoked salmon you'll ever taste. Quieter than the outdoor market — warmer, more curated, wonderful. Get the lohikeitto — Finnish salmon soup. Don't rush.
📍 Eteläranta 1 · South Harbour
Anna will love this
Gourmet Food Hall · City Centre
Stockmann Herkku
The legendary basement food hall of Helsinki's grand Stockmann department store. An Aladdin's cave of Finnish and international delicacies — artisan cheeses, cured meats, fresh bread, Finnish chocolates, cloudberry products, and every kind of Nordic ingredient you'd want to bring home. Helsinki's finest food shopping under one roof.
📍 Aleksanterinkatu 52 · Basement floor
