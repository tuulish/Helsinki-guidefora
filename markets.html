Guide · Markets
The Markets
🐟 · Taste Helsinki · Strawberries, smoked fish, cardamom buns, pickled things
Taste
Helsinki's markets
are alive.

The open-air market on the South Harbour has been here since the city's earliest days, and it still feels essential. In June it's at its peak — strawberries from Finnish farms, smoked whitefish wrapped in paper, vendors calling out in Finnish and Swedish and tourist-English.

The indoor Old Market Hall nearby is different — more refined, but no less vital. Cheese, bread, reindeer meat, cloud berry jam. Come hungry. Stay as long as you want.

Open-Air Market · Harbour
Kauppatori · Market Square
The great open square at the water's edge. Stalls selling Finnish strawberries, salmon, smoked fish, cloudberry jam, and every shade of pastry. In June it's in full swing, the Baltic glittering behind it. Buy something, eat it standing, absorb the scene. Mornings are best.
Must Visit
🏛️
Indoor Market Hall · Since 1889
Vanha Kauppahalli
The Old Market Hall, a red-brick beauty built in 1889. Inside: cheese merchants, fishmongers, spice sellers, a beloved oyster bar, and some of the best smoked salmon you'll ever taste. It's quieter than the outdoor market — warmer, more curated, wonderful. Get the lohikeitto, Finnish salmon soup.
Anna will love this
🥬
Weekend Market · Hakaniemi
Hakaniemi Market Hall
A little less touristy, a little more local. Two floors — fresh produce downstairs, textiles and handicrafts above. The kind of place real Helsinkians shop on Saturday mornings. Grab something from the hot food counter and find a stool upstairs.
