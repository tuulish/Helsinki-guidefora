Finds
Finnish design is
the real thing.

Helsinki is a design city in the truest sense — it is not a marketing label. The names are familiar even outside Finland: Marimekko, Iittala, Artek. But the city also has a layer of smaller, independent shops that reward wandering. The Punavuori neighbourhood (also called "Design District Helsinki") is where most of them live.

Bring a bigger bag. Or ship things home. Buy the Finnish linen kitchen towel, the Iittala bowl, the jar of cloudberry jam. These are things you will actually use.

Flagship · Esplanadi
Marimekko
The Esplanadi flagship store is the best place to see Marimekko as it should be — the full collections, the bold poppy prints, the summer dresses. Finnish design at its most joyful and unafraid. Even if you don't buy anything, the store itself is a pleasure. (You will buy something.)
Anna will love this
🫙
Finnish Design · Esplanadi
Iittala
Glass and ceramic designed to last a lifetime. Iittala's Helsinki store stocks the full range — the iconic Aalto vase, the Kastehelmi bowls, the Teema plates. Beautiful objects that are actually meant to be used. A proper Finnish souvenir that isn't a reindeer toy.
Design Classic
🏪
Neighbourhood · South Helsinki
Punavuori Design District
The neighbourhood south of the city centre where independent design shops cluster. Concept stores, ceramics studios, vintage furniture, Finnish linen, art galleries, and the kind of shop with one beautiful thing in the window and ten more inside. Walk slowly. Allow detours.
Neighbourhood wander
📚
Bookshop · Keskusta
Academic Bookstore
Alvar Aalto's building, home to one of the best bookshops in Northern Europe. The English section is excellent. Buy something to read in the park. The building's atrium and skylight alone make it worth the stop. Café Aalto is inside if you need a break between shelves.
Lovely detour
🎨
Concept Store · Kamppi
Artek
The furniture company founded by Alvar Aalto in 1935, still producing the same iconic designs. The store is itself beautifully designed. The furniture is beautiful but expensive — the accessories and smaller objects are more approachable. Finnish modernism in object form.
Finnish Icon
✨
Vintage · Kallio
UFF & Vintage Shops · Kallio
The Kallio neighbourhood has a strong vintage scene — charity shops, second-hand design finds, old Finnish ceramics. The UFF shops often have surprising Marimekko pieces and old Iittala at fraction of retail. Friday afternoons are particularly good. Bring cash, bring patience.
