Finds
Finnish design is
the real thing.

Helsinki is a design city in the truest sense. The names are familiar even outside Finland: Marimekko, Iittala, Artek. But the city also has a layer of smaller, independent shops that reward wandering. The Punavuori neighbourhood — Design District Helsinki — is where most of them live.

Bring a bigger bag. Buy the Finnish linen kitchen towel, the Iittala bowl, the jar of cloudberry jam. These are things you will actually use.

"The Design District Helsinki map is free — pick one up at any design shop or hotel. It lists around 200 shops, studios and galleries in a walkable area. Use it."
Neighbourhood · South Helsinki
Punavuori Design District
The neighbourhood south of the city centre where independent design shops cluster. Concept stores, ceramics studios, vintage furniture, Finnish linen, art galleries, and the kind of shop with one beautiful thing in the window and ten more inside. Walk slowly. Allow detours. This is not a place you rush through.
📍 Punavuori · South of city centre

Bookshop · Aalto Building · Keskusta
Academic Bookstore
Alvar Aalto's building, home to one of the best bookshops in Northern Europe. The English section is excellent — buy something to read in the park. The building's atrium and skylight alone make it worth the stop. Café Aalto is inside if you need a break between shelves. One of those places where you go for one thing and stay much longer.

Vintage · Kallio
UFF & Vintage Shops · Kallio
The Kallio neighbourhood has a strong vintage scene — charity shops, second-hand design finds, old Finnish ceramics. The UFF shops often have surprising Marimekko pieces and old Iittala at a fraction of retail price. Friday afternoons are particularly good for fresh stock. Bring cash, bring patience, bring an open mind.
