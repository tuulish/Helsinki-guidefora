The Sights
🏛️ · See Helsinki · Icons, surprises, and the city's quiet wonders
See
Helsinki is full
of surprises.

The city isn't a place of overwhelming monuments — it works differently. The neoclassical cathedral on its hilltop square. The Russian Orthodox church glowing red on a rise above the harbour. A church literally carved into the bedrock of the city. These are not things you just walk past.

Helsinki's architecture is a conversation between empires and eras — and in June, with the light lasting almost all night, everything looks a little more beautiful than it has any right to.

The icons
Helsinki's wonders
✦ Curator's note
"Walk up to Senate Square at golden hour — roughly 9pm in late June. The cathedral turns honey-coloured and the square empties of tourists. Stand there for a while."
🕍
Neoclassical · Senate Square
Helsinki Cathedral
The great white cathedral at the top of Senate Square — Helsinki's most iconic image. Built in the 1850s, its green dome visible from the harbour. Walk up the broad stairs, look across the square, look back at the sea. This is the heart of the city. Simple, majestic, worth every visit.
Icon
⛪
Orthodox · Katajanokka
Uspenski Cathedral
Red brick and golden onion domes, the largest Orthodox cathedral in Western Europe. Sitting above the harbour on a small rocky hill, it's dramatic from every angle. Inside: gold icons, candlelight, incense. Something of old Russia preserved in this Northern city. Quiet and remarkable.
Anna will love this
🪨
Modernist · Töölö
Temppeliaukio Church
The Rock Church — blasted directly into the granite bedrock of the city, with a copper-wire ceiling that lets in daylight. One of Finland's most visited sites, and one of its most genuinely extraordinary. The acoustics are legendary; concerts are held here. Even if you skip it, you should go.
Unmissable
🏛️
Public Square · City Centre
Senate Square
Helsinki's grand ceremonial space — the cathedral above, the government palace on one side, the university on another. In the centre, a statue of Tsar Alexander II. It's a strange, stately place that feels slightly out of time. Beautiful in any light, extraordinary in the June evening gold.
Heart of the City
🎨
Design Museum · Punavuori
The Design Museum
Finland's national design collection — Marimekko fabrics, Iittala glass, Aalto furniture. A crash course in why Finnish design became a world reference. The building itself is a beautiful 1890s school. A good rainy-morning option, or just because design history is genuinely fascinating here.
Design lover's pick
🌊
Outdoor · Sibelius Park
Sibelius Monument
A forest of 600 hollow steel pipes in a park by the sea — an abstract tribute to Finland's greatest composer. Odd, beautiful, very Finnish. The surrounding park, Sibelius Park, runs down to the water. A lovely walk in any direction. Go, be confused, appreciate it.
