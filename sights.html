The Sights
🏛️ · See Helsinki · Icons, surprises, and the city's quiet wonders
See
Helsinki is full
of surprises.

The city isn't a place of overwhelming monuments — it works differently. The neoclassical cathedral on its hilltop square. The Russian Orthodox church glowing red above the harbour. A church carved directly into the bedrock. These are not things you just walk past.

Helsinki's architecture is a conversation between empires and eras — and in June, with the light lasting almost all night, everything looks a little more beautiful than it has any right to.

The icons
Helsinki's wonders
✦ Curator's note
"Walk up to Senate Square at golden hour — roughly 9pm in late June. The cathedral turns honey-coloured and the square empties of tourists. Stand there for a while."
Modernist · Blasted from Rock · Töölö
Temppeliaukio Church
The Rock Church — blasted directly into the granite bedrock of the city, with a copper-wire ceiling that lets daylight pour in. One of Finland's most visited sites, and genuinely one of its most extraordinary. The acoustics are legendary; concerts are held here. Odd, beautiful, very Finnish. Even if you're not religious — go. You'll remember it.
📍 Lutherinkatu 3 · Töölö
Unmissable
Neoclassical · Senate Square
Helsinki Cathedral
The great white cathedral at the top of Senate Square — Helsinki's most iconic image. Built in the 1850s, its green dome visible from the harbour. Walk up the broad stairs, look across the square, look back at the sea. This is the heart of the city. Simple, majestic, worth every visit — especially in the long golden June evening light.
📍 Unioninkatu 29 · Senate Square
Icon
Museum · Punavuori
The Design Museum
Finland's national design collection — Marimekko fabrics, Iittala glass, Aalto furniture. A crash course in why Finnish design became a world reference. The building itself is a beautiful 1890s school. A lovely rainy-morning option, or simply because design history is genuinely fascinating when it's this good. The gift shop alone is worth the trip.
